Flood Precautions & Safety Tips

Floods can happen anywhere and at any time. Being prepared and knowing what to do before, during, and after a flood can save lives and property.

Before a Flood

  • Know the safest evacuation route from your home to high, safe ground.
  • Prepare and maintain a basic emergency supply kit (water, food, flashlight, batteries, medications, important documents).
  • Sign up for local emergency alerts and weather warnings.
  • Protect important documents in waterproof containers or digital copies.
  • Move valuables to higher levels and clear drains/gutters.
  • Purchase or renew flood insurance if available in your area.

During a Flood

  • Evacuate immediately if authorities instruct you to do so.
  • Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters. Turn Around, Don’t Drown!
  • Stay off bridges over fast-moving water.
  • If trapped in a building, go to the highest level—avoid closed attics.
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for instructions and updates.

After a Flood

  • Return home only when authorities say it is safe.
  • Avoid driving except in emergencies.
  • Beware of snakes and other animals that may have entered your home.
  • Wear heavy gloves and boots during clean-up.
  • Do not touch electrical equipment if it is wet or you are standing in water.
  • Avoid wading in floodwater, which may be contaminated or electrically charged.
